A Guide to Understanding IPOs in India

Initial Public Offerings (IPOs) are an exciting opportunity for investors to be part of a company's growth story from its early days on the stock market.

An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is the process by which a private company becomes a public company by offering its shares to the public for the first time. It's a significant milestone for any company, providing access to capital for growth and expansion.

Key IPO Terminology:

  • Draft Red Herring Prospectus (DRHP): A detailed document filed with SEBI that provides information about the company's business, financials, and the IPO.
  • Price Band: The price range within which investors can bid for the company's shares.
  • Lot Size: The minimum number of shares an investor must apply for in an IPO.
  • Subscription/Bidding Period: The period during which investors can apply for the IPO shares.
  • Listing Day: The day the company's shares start trading on the stock exchange.

How to Apply for an IPO:

  1. Demat Account: You must have a Demat account to apply for an IPO.
  2. ASBA (Applications Supported by Blocked Amount): Most IPO applications are made through ASBA, where the application amount is blocked in your bank account until the shares are allotted.
  3. Bidding: You can place your bid within the price band through your broker's platform or your bank's net banking portal.
  4. Allotment: If the IPO is oversubscribed, the allotment of shares is done through a lottery system.

Investing in IPOs can be rewarding, but it also comes with risks. It's important to do thorough research by reading the DRHP, understanding the company's business model, and considering its valuation before investing.
